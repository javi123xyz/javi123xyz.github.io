Javier's Select Academic Works


Throughout my collegiate career both at Allan Hancock College (2015 - 2017) and UC Irvine (2017 - 2019) , I have had to write numerous essays, create various infographics, and compose several research reports for class requirements. I believe that writing samples and assingments are often a better indicator of comprehension and dexterity in a subject matter than letter grades are. I take pride in the work that I do for academic purposes and I believe that it shows clearly in its quality. I chose the following assingments because I believe they accurately showcase my versatility as a writer, my synthesis skills, and also my intellectual and analytical growth as a scholar.


This assingment was for my Introduction to Comparative Politics class that I took during my time at community college. Our instructions were to pick one of the five countries we covered throughout the course of the class and construct a map infographic of the country that indicated its major manufacturing centers and location of ethnic or religious minorities. We presented the infographic in class and talked briefly about our country's political environment and challenges.

This was similar to the previous paper, but we had to chose a different country and write a more in-depth paper regarding the country's political history, citizen participation, and a personal prediction for how we think they will do in the future. The paper is on the right.

For this paper, we were asked to take one country among the top twenty richest in the world and one among the top twenty poorest in the world and compare their geopolitical and economic situations, human rights records, and each country's relationship with the United States. I chose Qatar and Nepal.

This is a book review of Ann Garrel's Putin Country: A Journey into the Real Russia. I use the book to construct an analysis of the state of Russian society, politics, and economics under Vladimir Putin's leadership.

This was my final paper for my introduction to sociology class. By drawing from our own personal experience as well as the information and readings we covered in class, we were tasked with determining whether or not the American Dream is still accesible in modern times.

This assingment instructed us to play the role of an entry-level analyst for a multinational oil company. We had to chose a major oil-producing nation and write an assesment of whether or not it would be wise to invest in the country's oil sector. This assesment included an analysis of the country's history and politics, their exports and other economic measures, and required us to make our own original charts and graphs to support our assesment. I chose the Latin American country of Venezuela for my assesment.

This assingment is similar to the one above, but with some slight differences. We once again assesed a major oil-producing nation (this time, the African country of Nigeria) and determined whether or not the country was afflicted by the so-called "oil curse" and the policy steps which they can take to remedy it. We had to do extensive research on the country's history, politics, and current events and had to compile our own charts and graphs. Both this and the previous assingment were completed for a class on politics and oil.

For my United States foreign policy class, we were tasked with creating a policy memo recommendation to the president. In order to do so, we assumed the role of National Security Director of the United States and had to develop and present a cohesive policy plan to address the threat of a nuclear North Korea.

This essay was also for my United States foreign policy class. Using our own experience and the class material, we had to write an analysis of what "American Exceptionalism" is and the ways in which this ideology have shaped American politics and foreign policy.


This assingment was for my International Political Economy of the Middle East and North Africa class. To make our analysis, we had to create an annotated bibliography composing of academic and public media sources and condense the information into a brief one page summary.

For my Research Computing in the Social Sciences class, we had to write an APA-style research paper. Our only constraints were that the topic had to be related to technology in a clear way. For my Donald Trump Foreign Policy class, we were once again asked to present a policy recommendation, this time on how to curtail China's increasing influence on global politics and re-assert the United States as the reigning world hegemon.

This assingment was for my International Political Economy of East Asia class. It is actually a two-part paper. The first half is an essay which assesses how viable it is for contemporary developing countries to adopt the East Asian model of socioeconomic development. The second part is a brief outline which touts the strengths and weaknesses ofthe East Asian model compared to the alternatives of the so-called Washington Consensus. During spring quarter, I took a United States Foreign Policy in East Asia class (USFPEA) which required extenisve reading and writing. Before every class, two students would have to write a President's Daily Brief where we would briefly summarize the top U.S., East Asian, and world news that occured between each class meeting. We would present the daily brief at the start of every class and have a brief discussion with our classmates and professor.

In this Presidential Daily Brief, my partner and I focused heavily on the developments of the joint United States - Democratic Republic of Korea Summit which had not taken place yet. We also also discussed the trade war between China and the United States, which was still brewing and not yet fully realized at the time.

Our USFPEA class was divided into three different groups, representing the U.S. Department of State, Department of Commerce, and Department of Defense. Our goal was to write an extensive report which outlined American interests and background issues in the Southeast Asia region. This report served as a basis for a policy simmulation our class conducted towards the end of the quarter. I was part of the Department of State and was in charge of the "Regional Issues" section of the report as well as parts of "Economic Institutions and Trade" section, namely the "Asia Development Bank," "Trans-Pacific Partnership," and "Economic Challenges" subsections. I also editted the report for grammar and diction, conciseness, and overall structure.

In this report (also for my USFPEA class), we took the role of a United States trade representative and analyzed the regional implications a trade war between the United States and China would have on our partners and allies as well as on the global economic system. I co-authored the "Consequences" subsection and once again provided editting for the final report.

For our USFPEA final, our class was divided in half, with one half representing the United States government and the other representing the Chinese government. We were further divided into subsections representing the different state departments or ministries of each country and I was assinged to the Chinese Ministry of Commerce. We wrote a report about our stance on several US-Chinese disputes, including the trade deficit, North Korea's nuclear threat, the South China Sea, and the issue of Taiwanese sovereignty. We discussed this in our final class with the American team to try and come up with mock policy proposals that would satisfy both sides. Though we came to some agreement on North Korea, we had major disagreements on the issue of intellectual property, the trade deficit, and tariffs.

For my American culture class, I wrote a literature review-style research paper on American food culture. I explored the history of American food and eating habits, the effects of governmental policy on food culture, and popular media depictions of food and the socialization of children into American food culture, among other topics. I don't want to spoil it too much, so feel free to read the introduction, or at least the abstract. I'm not one to typically brag, but this is perhaps the best work I have produced thus far. I put in a lot of reading, writing, and brain racking to put it all together and it paid off, as I received a 49/50 and high praise from my professor for it.
