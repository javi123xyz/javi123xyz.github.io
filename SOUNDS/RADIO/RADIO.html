KUCI 88.9 FM Radio Reports




During the 2017-2018academic year, I had the privilege of working as an intern with UCI's student radio news team. Every week, I would produce a new, professional-quality radio segment that took an in-depth look at a specific topic or story, ranging from domestic politics to international events, environmental news to pop culture, and everything in between. The segments would air weekly on the Pulp News show on 88.9 FM. Producing these segments often required extensive research, attending events to gather soundbytes and talk to people, and communicating with professors and other experts for interviews. Aside from helping me better develop my narrative and journalistic skills, this internship also taught me how to use professional-grade audio equipment as well as audio engineering software such as Adobe Audition, Audacity, and Ableton.

Northern California Wildfires - October 23, 2017

California and wildfires, unfortunately, go together like fine wine and cheese. The state suffers from a bout of destructive fires nearly every year and 2017 was no different. In this episode, I discuss the effects of climate change in fueling these fires, the destruction they caused, and what steps are being taken to mitigate future fire risks.


Catalonia's Quest for Independece - October 30, 2017

In this episode, I discussed the semi-autonomous Spanish region of Catalonia. In particular, I covered the independence movement which intensified in late 2017 after the passage of a popular referendum that would official declared the region's secession from Spain. My friend Joel, who studied abroad in Barcelona, the heart of Catalonia, joined me this episode and talked about his experience living in the region during the latest flare-up of the Catalan independence movement.


Affordable Housing Crisis in Irvine - November 6, 2017

Property values in California are some of the highest in the nation, contributing greatly to the state's ongoing housing insecurity crisis. This problem is greatly pronounced in the Orange County, where rent and mortgage prices greatly outweigh the average worker's wages. In this episode, I discuss the opening of Irvine's first affordable appartment complex, Anton Portola, and what it means for Irvine and the surrounding communities. David Snow, distinguished professor of sociology, joined me that week to discuss the extent of the Orange County's housing crisis best approaches to alleviate it.

Robert Mugabe Out as Zimbabwean President - November 27, 2017

Robert Mugabe, long-time dicator of the African country of Zimbabwe, stepped down from power on November 27, 2017 after nearly four decades in power. Mugabe's reign was marred with human rights abuses, corruption, and electoral fraud. I discuss the impact that Mugabe had on the counry of Zimbabwe and post-colonial Africa in general and what his retirement will mean for the country.


Turmoil in Honduras - December 4, 2017

The 2017 Honduran general election was marred with controversy and violence. Many Honduran and international election watchdogs were alarmed at the various instances of election fraud and voter suppression that took place. Protests broke out against incumbent President Juan Orlando Hernández, who defied the Honduran constitution by running (and winning) a second term in office. Protestors criticized Orlando Hernández for his alleged corruption and state-sponsored violence. I discuss the country's history of political turmoil and make some predictions on how this upheaval will fuel migration out of the country.

Chinese Hip Hop - February 5, 2018

Rap and hip hop grew from the fringes of society to become major staples of American culture. This dominance of the genre is not contained to the United States, however. Other countries have also developed their own versions of hip hop which borrows from American culture and incorporates local sounds. China, in particular, has developed quite the fledging hip hop scene. However, in early 2018, the Chinese government began to crack down on Chinese hip hop and rappers, taking down their online videos and even threatening entertainers with imprisonment. I discussed the spread of hip hop culture to China and their government's efforts to censure it and manifestations of other outside cultures. I also highlight how this mirrored efforts by the United States government to censure "gangsta" rap artists like N.W.A.

The topic of police brutality is one that . The Baltimore Police Department, in specific, has been one of the most notorious police departments in the country, with several allegations of evidence fabrication, brutality, drug dealing, extortion, and other improper behaviors plauging the department. Cryptocurrencies & the Environment - February 19, 2018

Cryptocurrency markets exploded in 2018 following the dramatic rise in bitcoin prices in later 2017/early 2018. Cryptocurrencies became the newest hot topic of discusssion, with major crypto proponents promising that blockchain technology would revolutionize our monetary system and the very fundamentals of how we live. Yet one of the aspects of cryptocurrensies that is often overlooked is its impact on the envirnonment.
